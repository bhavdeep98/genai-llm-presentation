# Research & Source Standards

## Source Hierarchy (use in this order)
1. **Original research papers** (arXiv, peer-reviewed journals)
2. **Official technical reports** (OpenAI, Google, Meta AI, Anthropic)
3. **Verified benchmarks** (MMLU, HumanEval, HELM, etc.)
4. **Official documentation** (API docs, model cards)
5. **Reputable technical blogs** (authors who are researchers or engineers at labs)
6. **Community analysis** (only if backed by reproducible evidence)

## Citation Format
Every claim must include:
- Paper title and year
- Authors (first author + et al. for 3+)
- ArXiv ID or DOI when available
- Direct quote or paraphrase clearly marked

## Hype vs. Reality Checklist
Before including any claim, verify:
- [ ] Is there a peer-reviewed paper or technical report?
- [ ] Are benchmarks reproducible or independently verified?
- [ ] Does the claim come from the creators (potential bias)?
- [ ] Are there known limitations acknowledged?
- [ ] Has this been replicated by independent researchers?

## Visual Standards
- All diagrams must have clear labels and attribution
- Statistics must show source, date, and sample size
- Comparisons must use consistent metrics
- Architecture diagrams should show data flow clearly

## What to Flag as Hype
- Claims without papers or reproducible benchmarks
- "X will replace Y" statements without evidence
- Cherry-picked benchmarks that don't reflect real usage
- Marketing material disguised as research
- Social media claims that misrepresent papers
